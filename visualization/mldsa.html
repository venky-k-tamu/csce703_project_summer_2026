Rounding & hint-bit mechanism (FIPS 204 §7.4, Alg 35–40)

Decompose

r = r1·(2γ2) + r0 with r0 ∈ (−γ2, γ2] — splits w into a coarse "high bits" commitment (w1) and a fine LowBits remainder, every Sign attempt.

Power2Round

Same idea with bucket width 2^D = 8192 instead of 2γ2 — used once at KeyGen to split t into public t1 and secret t0.

MakeHint / UseHint

A signature never reveals w's low bits. The signer sends one bit per coefficient, h = [HighBits(r) ≠ HighBits(r+z)]; the verifier reconstructs HighBits(w) from t1, z, c, and h alone.

UseHint(MakeHint(z,r), r) == HighBits(r+z) for |z| ≤ γ2 — the load-bearing identity that lets verification work without disclosing w's low bits.
